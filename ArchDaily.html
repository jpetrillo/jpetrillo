    ArchDaily is the world’s most visited architecture news and review blog, but the current brand and website design don’t reflect that. Revitalizing a dated and irrelevant brand, the new ArchDaily identity revolves around a responsive logo, a mark that visualizes key points of data in a project review, the content that ArchDaily emphasizes most.

    The new ArchDaily website takes inspiration from the modern and structural mark, borrowing from the visual languages of squares, blueprints, and construction. The new identity uses GT America (with its monospace family), a hybrid between a classic American Gothic and a timeless European Grotesque, a fitting representation of an international design publication.
